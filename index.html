Protocol case study

Case Study Sections

My Role

I’m the lead product designer for this project. I collaborated with UI/UX designers (Atif and Rifat) and software architect (Rohit) throughout this project.

Problem

Since 2014, Email was the primary communication and task delegation method for Chaldal employees and Managers. Here are the core problems with being dependent on emails

#1 Delayed Action

When an email is sent, some employees aren't responding promptly. Late response causes action or decision to be delayed by >1 day.

#2 Following up on a task

If you are one of the top management professional, following up on a task that has been assigned to a department gets more complicated.

#3 Information Asymmetry

Sending email to multiple department to take their necessary action creates information asymmetry and confusion among inter departments on their priority.

#4 Ambiguous Priority

Assigning priority on an email isn't easy which makes the department assign their own priority on a task.

USERS

Before we started designing, we deep dive into exiting behavioral and task management data of our business units to understand better. We also conducted series of customer interviews. We focused on identifying the pattern of task management habit of our employees.

We defined 3 user archetypes, and mapped them to their respective jobs-to-be-done.

Efficiency Expert

Top-tier user who has multiple business unit/departments under them. Mostly overviews performance and strategic operations.

Team Manager

Mid-tier user who has multiple users under them. Manages tasks that is set by the top-tier user and has regular routine tasks.

Finisher

Mostly end users who are mainly working on a task. Focused on the tasks at hand.

Design Sprints

We conducted design sprints to facilitate collaboration cross-departments. Product designers, Product managers and Creatives contributed their fresh ideas in this sprint. The purpose of these sprints is to align everyone on the same goal – To create a product that solves task management problem for each user.

User Flows

We mapped each archetype to their user journey on the app, with their respective success metrics.

Early Designs

A sneak peek into my early mid-fidelity designs and drafts. The designs have went through at least 10-15 iterations per screen. It is due to several reasons: Change in business direction, shift in product roadmap or simply to improve user experience.

Here’s a detailed walkthrough of the website.

Looks new. Feels like Professional.

Before the redesign, the website was cluttered, it had a lack of focus on Protocol’s core products. Now, Protocol has a fresh new modern look and a better experience. To improve our brand and product perception, we introduced new, scalable components, redesigned micro-interactions and repositioned the product.

Three segmented view for handling tasks faster.

1. Left Nav: On top, the room/department selection. Each room has a moderator/manager. Followed by notification, my tickets, follow up of any assigned tickets and report functions are located. Left navigation is collapsible, which gives the user the ability to focus on his task alone. Also, each room can create tags or categories for management of their tasks in the room.

2. Focus view: In the middle section, main tasks or the wider view of any task is opened.

3. Right nav: For modifying view settings. Sort and filter options are location on the right side, giving prompt access to the user for finding any task easily.
